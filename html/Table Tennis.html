Table Tennis


    Introduction

    As we all know, how important it is to associate one with some sporting activity to lead a healthy lifestyle and maintain a healthy physically active body. Table Tennis is one such activity which gives us an opportunity to lead just the life we all want to lead, a healthy and physically active one. It is played with wooden bats with rubber on their either sides usually of red and balck color distinctively which they use to hit a plastic ball which serves as the match decider.
    As the name itself suggests, the game is played on the table and is a small, faster and more agility requiring form of tennis. While tennis is about lengthy pick-ups and long runs, table tenis is about instinctiveness and short puts. The game is more faster, quicker and harder than tennis. A game for 2 or 4 players where 2 players play against each other and 4 players play as teams against the other. The rules are very clear and hold some resemblance to tennis, i.e. -
    - Keep the ball on the table or else the other guy gets the point.
    - Two bounces on your side of the table and you lose a point to the other guy.
    - Ball touches the net while serving results in re-serve.

    These were some of the basic rules of table tennis. Now, we shall know more about the game.


    Indian aspect and levels in the game

    Table Tennis has it's foot settles in India just fine. Although it is not as popular as cricket and other games, it still holds a significant value to India and contribute to the country's bright future in the internationally renowned games like Olympics, Common Wealth, Asian games etc. There is a federation in India working solely for the development of the game and for the ease of access in the international tournaments as forementioned, known as Indian Table Tennis Federation or ITTF. ITTf is working rigorously for the betterment of the game in India.
    ITTF organises events and tournaments for different age groups(under 13, under 16 etc) at diffeerent levels. There is a level of heirarchy in the game as -
    - School Level
    - City Level
    - District Level
    - State Level
    - Regional Level
    - National Level


    Renowned Indian Players and Achievements

    Althouh India is not on the heights of greatness in Table Tennis. in the recent days, it has started to make a stronger foot in the game.

      1. Sharath Kamal

      Sharath Kamal is arguably the best professional table tennis player India has ever produced. Current ranked at 30, he has won won the Senir National Championship nine times. he is also the winner of US Open Table Tennis Men's championship held at Grand Rapids, Michigan in July 2010 and won Gold medal at 2006 Commonwealth games at Melbourne. The government ha also honoured him with the fourth highest civilian award, Padma Shri.

      Manika Batra is one of the best Indian propsects in table tennis at 2020 Tokyo Olympics. She is, currently, the tp-ranked female table tennis player in India with 47 thrank in the world.
